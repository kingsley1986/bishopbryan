About the Founder:

Bishop Oriel Bryan

40 Years in the Ministry

Oriel Bryan the Second oldest of six siblings was born in Birmingham England to the late Bishop And Mother Bryan.He was also blessed as a baby by the late Bishop Sydney Dunn. At the age of nine he migrated to Canada on April 19th 1966. On the Queen Elizabeth ship they spent nine days on the ocean with his mother and six siblings. He got baptized at the age of 16 but never truly felt the calling of God. Until one day at the age of 18 in a Night club, he received a strong conviction from the Lord that change his life forever.

Soon after that he was filled with the power of the Holy Spirit. He played a very active role in his father’s church as group leader for the young people department. Later on he became the president of the young people department and built it from 15 to 80 young people on fire for God. He was also very active in High school and was also the athlete of the year. He was also the valedictorian at his graduation. He also had big dreams that one day he could make it to the NBA, however he made the All-Star team in Toronto in basketball and he also made the junior Olympic team in basketball for Canada. Bad luck happed one dreadful day in York university where he cracked his ankle and that was the end of his career in basketball. However, God had other plans for him.

He went on to put together one of the largest gospel concert in Toronto. From there to be the founder of Toronto Mass Choir with 100 members and also went on to be the founder of Toronto Outreach Choir with 50 members. The choir sang all over Ontario In parks, in churches, in concerts and in homeless shelters. Through much sadness, disappointment and discouragement, and after the death of his father, the Lord literally called him to the Pastoral order. The first church he started with the help of the Lord and his family was called new Jerusalem Church of Jesus Christ. He started the church with just five members and just a little over a year they had a little over 100 members. After 10 years of pastoring there was lots of disagreements and arguing. The assistant pastor also left and encouraged other members to go with him. Pastor Bryan was so discouraged and disappointed, he decided to stop Pastoring. It was a very dark chapter in his life. At his lowest point he heard the voice of God saying “who will go” three times. He turned around and said “Lord send me I will go”. The following year he found himself in India and Africa and in the Caribbean preaching the gospel.

Five years ago he felt the calling to go back to Pastoring. He now Pastors a Church by the name of New Birth Deliverance Ministries. He now also branched out to launch an organization that the Lord has laid on his heart from a long time ago. The name of this new organization is New Birth Church of Christ International with over 400 churches worldwide. The Lord also has blessed him with a wonderful spiritual woman of God and four wonderful beautiful children. His father also told him years ago that one day he will step into his shoes, however his father did not live to see it happened. The late Bishop Bryan would have been proud to see his son become the presiding Bishop over a very large organization ‘New Birth Church of Christ International’.
